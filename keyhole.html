Info

            The Keyhole is a variation of the beginner's method, where instead of solving the entire bottom layer, 1 corner is left unsolved as the "keyhole". This unsolved corner allows 3 of the cube's middle layer edges to be solved with much less effort than the beginner's method allows, reducing solving times. Once the beginner's method is learned, this method is not difficult to pick up.

Opinion

            The keyhole method isn't a bad speedcubing method. For the amount of effort it takes to learn, versus the reduction in solving time, it's actually a very effecient method to learn. In fact, after beginner's learn the beginner's method fairly well, the keyhole method is often the next step into the world of speedcubing. It's slightly more conceptually difficult than the beginner's method, though less algorithmically vigorous, which is why the beginner's method is still a better choice for easy learning at the beginning.
