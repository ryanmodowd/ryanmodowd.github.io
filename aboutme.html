Mathematics

I found a love for mathematics during my first analysis course in my undergraduate program. Prior to this course I had experienced mathematical proofs in some degree through geometry and linear algebra, but I credit this course for opening my eyes to the world of proof-based mathematics. I simply could not get enough after that.

Nowadays my research area revolves around the intersection of three primary fields of mathematics.

  • Harmonic Analysis: The study of representing complicated functions on some domain by a composition of simpler, inter-related, functions.
  • Approximation Theory: The study of errors arising from estimating said complicated functions by only some of the constituent simple functions.
  • Machine Learning: the study of adaptive algorithms and tools with the aim to allow machines to “learn” about various complicated topics and assist our lives.

Specifically, I am working with my advisor, Professor Hrushikesh Mhaskar, to leverage ideas from harmonic analysis and approximation theory (as well as other fields!) in order to develop rigorous mathematical models for machine learning applications. Our work is focused on the estimation of unknown, noisy functions on unknown domains given finitely many data points sampled from those functions: a prime setting for machine learning. We are developing a paradigm that simplifies and elucidates the mathematical approach to machine learning with the intent to reduce computational training costs and provide more mathematical clarity and justification to algorithms. For more detailed information, visit my Research page!
